About Glaucoma

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, which is vital for good vision. This damage is often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye (intraocular pressure or IOP). Glaucoma is one of the leading causes of blindness for people over the age of 60.

It can occur at any age but is more common in older adults. Many forms of glaucoma have no warning signs, and the effect is so gradual that you may not notice a change in vision until the condition is at an advanced stage.

Because vision loss due to glaucoma can't be recovered, it's important to have regular eye exams that include measurements of your eye pressure so a diagnosis can be made in its early stages and treated appropriately.

How to Identify Glaucoma

Early Symptoms

  • Patchy blind spots in peripheral or central vision
  • Tunnel vision in advanced stages
  • Severe headache
  • Eye pain
  • Blurred vision
  • Halos around lights
  • Eye redness

Risk Factors

  • High intraocular pressure (IOP)
  • Age over 60
  • Family history of glaucoma
  • Diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure
  • Eye injuries
  • Long-term corticosteroid use
  • Thin corneas
  • Extreme near/far sightedness

Diagnostic Tests

  • Tonometry – measures IOP
  • Ophthalmoscopy – optic nerve exam
  • Perimetry – peripheral vision
  • Gonioscopy – drainage angle
  • Pachymetry – corneal thickness
  • OCT – Optical Coherence Tomography

Existing Monitoring Models

Goldmann Applanation Tonometry

The gold standard for IOP measurement, requiring clinical setup and trained professionals.

Rebound Tonometry

Portable probe-based measurement with brief corneal contact.

Non-contact Tonometry

Air-puff method, convenient for screening but less accurate.

Our IoT-Based Model

GlaucoGuard System

Our system uses an ESP32 microcontroller with ultrasonic sensing to provide non-invasive, real-time IOP monitoring.

Acoustic radiation force is used to calculate corneal deformation, which correlates with IOP. Data is securely transmitted to the cloud.

  • Real-time IOP monitoring
  • Cloud-based analysis
  • Remote access
  • Early spike detection
  • User-friendly web interface
  • Affordable healthcare solution
